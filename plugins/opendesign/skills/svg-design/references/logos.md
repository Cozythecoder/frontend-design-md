# Logo Design Reference

## Types of Logos

### 1. Wordmark
Text-only, distinctive typography.

Examples: Google, Coca-Cola, FedEx

### 2. Lettermark
Initials or single letters.

Examples: HBO, IBM, CNN

### 3. Pictorial Mark
Recognizable image/icon.

Examples: Apple, Twitter bird, Target bullseye

### 4. Abstract Mark
Geometric form without direct representation.

Examples: Nike swoosh, Pepsi globe, Adidas stripes

### 5. Mascot
Character/figure.

Examples: KFC Colonel, MailChimp monkey

### 6. Combination Mark
Text + symbol together.

Examples: Doritos, Burger King, Lacoste

### 7. Emblem
Text inside symbol/badge.

Examples: Starbucks, Harley-Davidson, NFL

## Design Process

### Step 1: Discovery
Understand brand:
- Industry/category
- Target audience
- Brand personality (bold, playful, serious, luxury)
- Competitors
- Unique value proposition

### Step 2: Mood Board
Present 3-4 directions:

**Direction A: Bold & Geometric**
- Strong shapes
- High contrast
- Angular or blocky
- Examples: Tesla, Beats

**Direction B: Organic & Crafted**
- Hand-drawn feel
- Natural shapes
- Warm colors
- Examples: Innocent, Burt's Bees

**Direction C: Minimal & Typographic**
- Clean lines
- Negative space
- Simple wordmarks
- Examples: Sony, Calvin Klein

**Direction D: Playful & Dynamic**
- Bright colors
- Movement
- Whimsical shapes
- Examples: MailChimp, Google

### Step 3: Sketch
- 20-30 quick sketches
- Explore all directions
- Don't refine yet

### Step 4: Refine
- Select 3 strongest concepts
- Vectorize
- Test at small sizes (16x16)
- Check black/white versions

### Step 5: Deliver
- Full color
- Black
- White (for dark backgrounds)
- Icon-only version (if combo mark)
- Favicon

## Technical Specs

### Safe Zone
Keep 10-20% padding around logo for clear space.

### Minimum Sizes
- Print: 0.5 inches (12mm)
- Digital: 24px height
- Favicon: 16x16, 32x32

### Formats
- **SVG**: Primary, scalable
- **PNG**: 500x500, 1000x1000 with transparency
- **JPG**: Only if no transparency needed

## Dark Variants

When logo uses dark colors, create light variant:

```svg
<!-- Light background -->
<svg class="logo-default">
  <path fill="#1E3A5F" .../>
</svg>

<!-- Dark background -->
<svg class="logo-inverted">
  <path fill="#4B8BBE" .../>  <!-- Lighter shade -->
</svg>
```

## Common Mistakes

- Too complex (won't scale)
- Too similar to competitors
- Trendy (will date quickly)
- No clear space defined
- Missing monochrome versions
- Ignoring small-size legibility
